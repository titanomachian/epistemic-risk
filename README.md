# epistemic-risk
## Experiments in Computational Risk-led Epistemology

Mostly models and stuff to make computational experiments with my PhD thesis main themes (Duncan Pritchard's anti-luck and anti-risk epistemology). Professor Pritchard probably wouldn't like any of this, given his preference for a modal account of risk instead of the probabilistic one (which I believe could be related to fields like formal epistemology, risk theory and so on). But I like code. And, while "exploring" for my research, I found out about John McCarthy's papers on the philosophical challenges of setting up an epistemic framework for artificial intelligence based on circumscriptive reasoning. And now I want to do stuff with something like Lisp, ~~so why not Clojure?~~ so why not try out something like Hylang, Kawa, Racket, Common Lisp or Scheme?
